## Research Question

Can LSTM-learned temporal representations of option-implied risk measures uncover latent market regimes that improve short-term realized volatility forecasts?

---

## Results Summary

| Model | RMSE | vs M1 |
|-------|------|-------|
| M1 — HAR-RV (baseline) | 0.000111 | — |
| M2 — HAR-RV + raw options | 0.000194 | +74.8% |
| M3 — HAR-RV + regimes | 0.000115 | +3.5% |

Three interpretable market regimes identified: **Low-rate**, **Low-vol**, **Crisis**.

- Crisis regime captures GFC (2008–2009), COVID (March 2020), and rate hike cycle (2022)
- M3 is practically as accurate as M1 during Crisis periods (+0.6%), suggesting regime labels carry their greatest value precisely under market stress
- M3 significantly outperforms M2 (DM p<0.001), confirming that regime structure captures nonlinear information that raw variables in OLS cannot transmit

## Methodology

**Pipeline overview:**

```
Raw data (VIX, VVIX, SKEW, SPREAD, FFR, RV)
        ↓
Sliding windows (60 days × 6 features)
        ↓
LSTM Autoencoder (unidirectional, latent_dim=3)
        ↓
Bottleneck vectors → K-means (k=3, train 2007–2018 only)
        ↓
Regime labels (Low-rate / Low-vol / Crisis)
        ↓
HAR-RV + regime dummies → M3
        ↓
Out-of-sample evaluation (2021–2024)
```

**Key design choices:**

- LSTM strictly unidirectional (`bidirectional=False`) — prevents look-ahead bias
- K-means fitted on 2007–2018 only, centroids frozen — prevents look-ahead in clustering
- StandardScaler fitted on 2007–2018 only — no data leakage
- Rolling window evaluation (756-day window) — strictly out-of-sample
- DM test with HAC correction (Newey-West, h=5) — accounts for overlapping forecast errors
- All random seeds fixed to 42 — fully reproducible
